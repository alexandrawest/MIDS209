I'm Walking More in Quarantine - Barely

Did I take more steps per day before or after the shelter-in-place order? First, looking at steps per day

Steps Per Week and Month

It may be easier to see in aggregate.
By Alexandra West
Code source material: My code source
Data source: My iPhone, using the Health app downloaded into a csv


It was my impression that I was walking a lot more in quarantine, especially with walking my dog three times per day.
However, at least in this graph there isn't a discernable difference until I delve into the summary statistics, at least looking solely at data from January-May. There are clearly complete outliers where I've taken a big hike (or walked all over London on March 2 - pre-quarantine). It's not a perfect data collection device since I have to remember to bring it with me (not a wearable device).

Problem Abstraction
What We have a csv (table) of two columns, date (Year-month-day) and steps taken (numerical). This data comes from a data export from the Health app on my iPhone.
Why This is a comparison over time of steps taken, the visual task is to discern the differences in scatterplot values over time.
How Marks: point and line, and channels: x and y axes, color only to highlight the line and no other purpose
insight 1 There's a clear trend of not moving around much on Saturdays, or intentionally leaving my phone at home on the weekends.
insight 2 Looking purely at averages, my average steps per day in January and February were in the high 3,000s, and in March, April, and May, average steps per day are between 4,500 and 5,000. So there is a clear difference, just not when we visualize it by day.
